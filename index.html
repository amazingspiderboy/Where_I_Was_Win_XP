📝 Welcome to "Where I Was" - XP Edition

This is an interactive art project inspired by my personal journey, styled like an old Windows XP desktop. Here’s how to explore:

  • Click icons on the desktop to open memory windows.
  • Drag windows around by their title bars.
  • Use the "Start" menu to access quick shortcuts.
  • Audio will auto-play when available — make sure sound is on!

Explore stories from Sri Lanka and Seattle, learn about my dreams and hobbies, and visit special places across Sri Lanka by clicking their icons.

Sigiriya X

Sigiriya, the Lion Rock Fortress, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It was once a royal palace and fortress built atop a giant rock in the 5th century.

🌍 View on Google Maps

Ella X

Ella is a beautiful hill town famous for tea plantations, waterfalls, and scenic train rides.

🌍 View on Google Maps

Galle Fort X

Galle Fort is a coastal fortress with Dutch-colonial architecture. It’s known for its lighthouse, cafes, and historic charm.

🌍 View on Google Maps

Temple of the Tooth X

The Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa) in Kandy houses a sacred relic — the tooth of the Buddha. It’s a deeply spiritual and symbolic place in Sri Lankan culture.

🌍 View on Google Maps

Jaffna X

Jaffna, located in northern Sri Lanka, is a region with rich Tamil heritage, temples, and a vibrant culture distinct from the south.

🌍 View on Google Maps

The Beach X

The beach in Sri Lanka wasn’t just sand and sea — it was warmth that wrapped around me like a familiar story. I remember chasing waves, eating fried snacks from vendors, and watching the sky turn pink as the sun dipped into the ocean.

It was the sound of laughter, of family, and of stillness. I didn’t need anything else. No noise from school, no pressure. Just the rhythmic comfort of water pulling at the shore like a lullaby I had heard since before I could understand it.

Hiking – Seattle X

Hiking around Seattle always gives me that rare feeling of stillness and motion at once. I love trails like Rattlesnake Ledge and Discovery Park — tall evergreens, misty air, and the occasional call of a distant bird.

Being above the city with muddy shoes, tired legs, and a racing heart — that’s how I know I’m alive.
